Chatka & Victory

Most of what is considered shastar today are built for decoration - not for warriors.

They are not heat treated, so they bend out of shape, do not sharpen, and if they do they do not retain the edge. They may even break, the handles chaffe the hand, and the overall piece is not balanced. The battle is lost long before it even begins

Some Sikhs go outside, and attempt to fill this gap by purchasing weapons from other traditions, such as Filipino Martial Arts, or Katanas.
Or, they buy antiques, many of which have been stripped of their heat treatment by being over-cleaned, and therefore won't hold an edge and will bend if it hits bone.

To us, this is a form of beadbi. At Fateh Forge, we set out to fix that.
Every shastar we create is battle ready - crafted through research, precision, and respect for tradition.

Our designs draw directly from antiques including the renowned Bikaner arsenal, original Sikh weapon catalogues, and historic manuscripts that defined true function and authenticity

Each weapon is forged and tested to withstand real training and chatka execution -
Blades that hold their edge
handles that stay true under rigour
Balance that flows with your movement
No dead weigth
This is not wall art
This is a living tradition
Reborn for warriors who still train, still fight, still remember.
